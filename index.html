Arab cuisine is a rich tapestry of flavors, ingredients, and culinary techniques that span across the Arab world, from the Maghreb in the west to the Mashriq in the east. This diverse cuisine has evolved over centuries, influenced by geography, culture, climate, and the exchange of spices and herbs along ancient trade routes. From the fragrant, herb-infused dishes of Lebanon to the rich, spiced tagines of Morocco, Arab food is known for its bold use of ingredients like olive oil, garlic, tahini, and fresh herbs. Each dish tells a story of the region’s history, its people, and their connection to the land and to each other.
